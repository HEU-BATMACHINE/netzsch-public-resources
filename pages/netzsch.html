ManufacturingComponent#

  • https://w3id.org/batteryequipment/public/ontology#ManufacturingComponent

Comment A component is a smaller part or unit within a larger system or piece of equipment. It typically has a specific role or function within the equipment but doesn't perform the overall task on its own. Examples include a processor in a computer, a gear in an engine, or a valve in a pump. Components are building blocks of equipment and must be integrated into a system to contribute to the equipment's overall function.

ManufacturingDevice#

  • https://w3id.org/batteryequipment/public/ontology#ManufacturingDevice

Comment Refers to a smaller, often more self-contained tool or machine that serves a specific function. Devices are typically simpler than equipment and are often more portable or singular in function. Example: A thermometer, smartphone, or a blood pressure monitor would be considered a device because they are relatively small and perform specific functions.

ManufacturingEquipment#

  • https://w3id.org/batteryequipment/public/ontology#ManufacturingEquipment

Comment Refers to larger systems or sets of tools used for a specific purpose, often involving multiple components working together. Equipment generally denotes something more complex or industrial, such as manufacturing machinery, medical systems, or laboratory apparatus. Example: A printing press or a medical imaging machine would be considered equipment, as they are larger, more complex setups used for industrial or professional applications.
